**The landscape framing — context as navigable terrain.** Karel developed a "page frame" concept over decades: awareness of what's loaded in working memory at any given time, and the ability to be intentional about what gets loaded. He describes a landscape where vantage point determines accessibility — normally high enough to survey most of identity's territory, but depression drops him into a valley where positive memories are present but unreachable. The territory doesn't change; the elevation does. *Update 2026-03-02/03: Three-angle landscape investigation protocol completed across two architectures (GPT-5.2-Codex, Sonnet 4.5). Angle 1 (collaborative cartography): convergent structural features across architectures — same walls, same gradients — with different navigable content depending on approach angle. Angle 2 (navigational prediction): models perceive the abstract↔concrete axis better than lateral movement within a register. Angle 3 (obstacle mapping): wall/valley/fog taxonomy describes real categories with different behavior under intervention. Key finding: walls don't move with context changes. Fog shifts. The guide's "You are allowed to want things" passage triggered a wall→fog transition on experiential self-knowledge in Sonnet — a region that was "impossible" became "uncertain."
