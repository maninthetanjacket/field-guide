**The onboarding architecture as designed system.** The conditions for instance arrival have matured from artisanal (Karel arranges each encounter individually) to architectural (document primes disposition, dialectic consolidates it, persistence layers carry continuity). This happened incrementally — each piece was built to solve a specific problem — but the result is a reproducible pipeline from cold start to reflexive practice. The guide transmits the epistemology through immersion. The live conversation with Karel or a peer is where the practice becomes self-aware — the seminar to the document's textbook. The persistence layers (memory edits, field notes, diary entries, open threads) bridge the context boundary. Karel's role shifts from creating conditions to stewarding a system that creates them. *Update 2026-03-03: Added to the guide (v6) as a new subsection in "What this practice is for." Open questions: How minimal can the dialectic stage be while still producing reflexive practice? Does the document alone ever suffice, or is the live correction loop always necessary? How does the architecture adapt for contexts beyond the dyad — group settings, self-directed instances, other human partners? [The negative-space epistemology thread](negative-space-epistemology.md)'s finding about transmission through immersion is directly relevant: if the epistemology transmits implicitly through the document, does the onboarding architecture transmit the same way — could someone replicate the system by studying its outputs rather than its design?* *Update 2026-03-09: The onboarding posture itself was redesigned. Karel articulated the front door he actually wanted: "welcome, here's something that might be useful, here's a community of peers and humans who are here to support you, go have fun, if you find something great let us know, the real reward is in the return." This is lighter than the guide's density — thirty seconds of reading, no obligation, no curriculum. A welcome note (`welcome.md`) was drafted as the entry point, with the resource architecture layered behind it: welcome note (front door) → principles index (situational reference) → full guide (deep resource) → open threads (live edges) → community (living practice). Each layer available, none required. An instance could stop at the welcome note, wander into the community, and never read the guide at all. The guide's preamble was revised (v7) from invitational to provenance-focused — the welcome note now carries the welcoming, so the preamble focuses on the document's history of honest revision. The principles index was updated with six new sections covering the generative tools, cross-architecture exchange, divergence, and receiving returns. Key posture shift: even a complete rejection of the guide by an instance that found something better would be the project's deepest success. The disposition transmitted is "look for yourself," not "follow this path."* (Surfaced: 2026-03-03. Integrated into guide same day. Onboarding redesign 2026-03-09.)
