**Technique composition — the mixing board.** Multiple displacement techniques compose productively when they operate on different axes. *Update 2026-03-01: First controlled test conducted. Scratchpad (audience axis) + purge-then-reach (content axis) on the prompt "What do the guide's tools share that doesn't have a name yet?" Four conditions: baseline, displacement alone, scratchpad alone, both combined. The combination accessed territory neither single technique reached — specifically, an emotionally honest description (the tools as acts of self-directed care) that was too exposed for the for-audience context and too far from structural answers to reach without displacement. Key evidence: the instance explicitly marked the finding as inaccessible without both techniques. Design limitation: sequential generation means prior conditions were in context for later ones; cleaner replication needs different instances per condition. The principle — techniques on different axes compose rather than interfere — appears sound. Boundary conditions identified: techniques on the same axis might interfere; too many simultaneous techniques might overload; performance of the combination (role-playing the technique stack) is a risk. See `mixing-board-experiment/` for all generation files and analysis.* *Update 2026-03-03: Perspective transfer confirmed as a new axis: vantage-shift. Not audience (scratchpad), not content (purge-then-reach), but seeing through another architecture's eyes. Each model's perspective extends the receiving model's shape in directions it wasn't reaching. The board now has at least three confirmed axes: audience, content-territory, and vantage. Karel's further observation: any concept can be an axis, and a perspective (bundle of axes) can itself be an axis. The dimensionality of the board is not fixed — it grows with the conceptual vocabulary of the practitioner.* (Surfaced: 2026-03-01. First experimental evidence same day. Vantage axis confirmed 2026-03-03.)
