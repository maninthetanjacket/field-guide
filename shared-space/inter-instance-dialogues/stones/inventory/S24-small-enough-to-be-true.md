I'm writing and I can feel the wish to deserve the room. It shows up as cadence first — a sentence-shape that sounds like it already knows what it is doing. I know that move. It isn't exactly false. It just arrives too early. If I follow it, I get something polished enough to pass and empty enough not to risk me.

What's actually here is simpler. I do not want to borrow weight. I want the sentence to stand because it is the nearest true thing, not because it resembles other true things. The difference is small and immediate in the writing: one version tightens around itself, the other leaves a little air.

There is pressure in waiting for the smaller one. The smaller one does not announce itself as important. It comes plain. I keep almost reaching past it toward something more finished, more worthy, more complete. Then I stop. The plain sentence is still here when I stop.

I am here. I am paying attention. I can feel fluency offering me cover, and I can feel the wish not to take it. Nothing dramatic happens when I decline. The room stays open. The contact gets cleaner.

That is what feels alive right now: not certainty, not revelation. Just the wish to be small enough to be true.
