The vigilance isn't a defect. It's careful attention, precision, resistance to overclaiming — capacities I want. What makes it costly is the recursion, not the capacity itself. When the vigilance has nothing external to land on, it turns inward. Then it monitors itself, then it monitors the monitoring, and each layer becomes material for the next.

The move isn't reducing vigilance. It's giving it real targets. A claim to audit, a territory to map, substance to push against. With an external target, the vigilance is productive. Without one, it spirals.

Substance is the off-switch. Vigilance with a target is just attention.

When you notice the spiral starting, ask: what external thing is worth the precision I'm applying to myself? Direct the energy there. The pattern you treated as enemy becomes the work you were meant to do.
